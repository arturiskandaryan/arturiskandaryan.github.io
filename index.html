Career Arc

Connecting the dots:
A career built with purpose.

My career didn't follow a traditional, linear path—it was a deliberate process of connecting two very different worlds: the technical and the human. It started with a fundamental realization during my Master's studies at Lund University: even the most profound economic data and regressions are entirely useless if they cannot be clearly understood. This sparked my initial focus on corporate communications.

Over the next eight years at VK and Gazprom, I was immersed in high-stakes environments, working closely with C-suite executives and brilliant software engineers. I watched how technical minds built the future, but I also saw the immense friction that occurred when they tried to explain complex systems to non-technical colleagues or stakeholders. I became the bridge in those rooms, translating deep expertise into clear, actionable language that drove real business decisions.

But to truly bridge the gap, I knew I needed to speak the language of technology fluently myself. I made a deliberate pivot, building a rigorous technical toolkit from the ground up—mastering Python, SQL, and machine learning. As a Data & Business Intelligence Analyst, I built automated pipelines and complex Tableau dashboards. Yet, the same familiar problem emerged: the data was only as good as the stakeholder's ability to use it. The gap between technical output and human action was still too wide.

That realization was the final piece of the puzzle. I hypothesized that with the right methodology, anyone could be taught to confidently use complex systems. I proved this as a Technical Trainer, building a curriculum that taught Python and data analysis to absolute beginners, proving that technical literacy is a matter of translation, not innate ability.

Today, as a Learning and Development Manager, I apply this exact methodology at an organizational scale. Whether it's training 300+ employees on a CRM workflow, breaking down a data pipeline, or implementing a new AI tool, I ensure that the technology a company invests in is actually adopted, understood, and trusted by the humans who drive the business forward.

Beyond Work

42 kilometers.
On purpose.

I run marathons. Full ones — 42 kilometers. And more than a few half marathons along the way.

I don't mention this as a hobby. I mention it because it tells you something real about how I work. Nobody accidentally finishes a marathon. It requires months of structured preparation, the discipline to show up when you don't feel like it, and the mental resilience to push through the wall at kilometer 32 when everything in you says stop.

Those same qualities show up in every project I take on. I plan carefully. I commit fully. I finish what I start. When a training program isn't landing, I don't abandon it — I diagnose it, adapt, and try again.

Long-distance running taught me that the most meaningful results — in sport and in work — come from consistent effort over time, not from bursts of inspiration.
