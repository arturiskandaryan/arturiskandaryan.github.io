The Bridge
I sit at the intersection of complex systems and the people who need to use them.

Most organizations invest heavily in AI tools, data infrastructure, and technical systems — then watch their people not use them. Not because the tools are bad. Because no one built the bridge.

I bring genuine technical depth — Python, SQL, ML, Tableau, HubSpot — combined with the ability to make any of it land with a non-technical audience. I've spent a decade on both sides of that gap and I know exactly what it takes to close it.

Over 10+ years across Armenia, Russia, Sweden, and the US, I've trained 300+ professionals — sales teams, engineers, C-suite executives — turning technical complexity into operational confidence and real business outcomes.

I'm at my best in roles that might say "Enablement" or "Technical Training" or "Product Education" — but where the real job is being the bridge between technology and the humans who need it.

Career Arc

A story built
with intent.

2008 — 2010
The Foundation
Lund University, Sweden · MSc Economic Growth & Innovation
My Master's thesis required testing an economic hypothesis using Sigma Convergence and regression analysis. The tools were different — but the thinking was identical: take a complex question, find the right data, and let the analysis tell the story honestly. This is where it all started.
2012 — 2020
The Tech Immersion
VK (VKontakte) · Moscow
Eight years inside one of Russia's largest tech companies — on-site three times a week. Personal relationships with CEO, CFO, CTO, and senior technical leadership. I wasn't an engineer. I was the person who brought their world to others. This is where the pivot began.
2014 — 2018
Executive Communication
Gazprom · Moscow
Trained C-suite executives for high-stakes international negotiations and board presentations. I learned that the most powerful professionals in any room still need someone who can help them communicate clearly. Expertise alone is never enough.
2018 — 2021
Building the Technical Toolkit
Independent Data Analyst · Freelance
The early analytical instinct from Lund became a serious technical skillset. Web scraping, statistical modeling, machine learning — real-world market analysis across real estate, automotive, and retail. Python became my second language.
2021 — 2022
The Problem Becomes Clear
Mantrac Russia · BI Analyst
I owned the full analytical pipeline — and kept noticing the same thing: the insights I produced weren't being used. Not because the analysis was wrong. Because the gap between technical output and human decision-maker was too wide. This is the problem I've spent the rest of my career solving.
2022 — 2024
Testing the Hypothesis
EduProf Training Centre · Technical Data Trainer
Could the right teaching approach make anyone capable of working with data? I designed a curriculum from scratch and tested it with 50+ adult learners per cohort, most with zero coding experience. The hypothesis was confirmed: anyone can learn to think with data if you explain it the right way.
2024 — Present
The Bridge, Fully Built
Crystalia Glass LLC · L&D Manager · New York Metro
Technical depth plus teaching methodology applied at organizational scale. 300+ employees trained across sales, engineering, and project management. HubSpot, technical specifications, engineering drawings — made understandable, adopted, and trusted by the humans who need to use them.

Beyond Work

42 kilometers.
On purpose.

I run marathons. Full ones — 42 kilometers. And more than a few half marathons along the way.

I don't mention this as a hobby. I mention it because it tells you something real about how I work. Nobody accidentally finishes a marathon. It requires months of structured preparation, the discipline to show up when you don't feel like it, and the mental resilience to push through the wall at kilometer 32 when everything in you says stop.

Those same qualities show up in every project I take on. I plan carefully. I commit fully. I finish what I start. When a training program isn't landing, I don't abandon it — I diagnose it, adapt, and try again.

Long-distance running taught me that the most meaningful results — in sport and in work — come from consistent effort over time, not from bursts of inspiration.
